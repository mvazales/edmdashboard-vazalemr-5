## Key Stakeholder Evidence from IBISWorld Industry Analysis

### Market Stakeholder Insights (Source: IBISWorld Beauty, Cosmetics & Fragrance Stores Report 2025)

**Industry Size & Growth Stakeholder Perspective:**
- Market size: $68.6 billion (2025)
- 5-year growth rate: 5.0% CAGR (2020-2025) 
- 454,000 employees across 167,000 businesses
- Strong economic recovery supporting consumer discretionary spending on beauty

**Consumer Stakeholder Behavior Evidence:**
- Beauty products viewed as both discretionary AND essential by different consumer segments
- Pandemic boosted performance as consumers invested in wellness and beauty routines
- Premiumization trend: customers willing to spend on luxury beauty products for long-term benefits
- Skincare segment particularly resilient during economic volatility

**Loyalty Program Stakeholder Evidence:**
- Major players (Sephora, Ulta) succeed due to "broad product range, expertise and loyalty programs"
- Customers value loyalty programs as key differentiator for beauty retailers
- Strong customer retention drives 80% of sales for leading beauty retailers

**Competitive Landscape Stakeholder Insights:**
- High and increasing competition level in beauty retail
- Online retailers disrupting traditional brick-and-mortar due to lower operating costs
- Proximity to population hubs critical for foot traffic and sales success
- Technology integration essential for competitive advantage

**Geographic Stakeholder Considerations:**
- Metropolitan areas generate highest foot traffic and sales
- Location proximity to target demographics directly impacts customer retention
- Regional market conditions significantly affect local business performance

**Economic Stakeholder Pressures:**
- Inflation impacts discretionary beauty spending
- Economic uncertainty causes consumers to shift spending priorities
- Per capita disposable income growth (2.2% CAGR through 2030) supports continued industry growth

**Industry Stakeholder Challenges:**
- Supply chain disruptions from potential tariffs on European imports
- Rising operational costs difficult to pass to price-sensitive consumers
- Need to balance online and brick-and-mortar customer experiences

## Consumer Stakeholder Evidence from Mintel Personal Care Report 2025

### Key Consumer Behavior Insights (Source: Mintel Personal Care Consumer US 2025)

**Premium Spending & Loyalty Patterns:**
- Consumers prioritize high spending on sunscreen, facial skincare, and bodycare
- Women 55+ are key drivers of facial skincare premium spending
- Men aged 35-54 and women under 55 represent future growth opportunities in facial skincare
- "Skinification" trend fuels demand for premium products across multiple categories

**Economic Sensitivity & Brand Loyalty:**
- Only 30% of financially stable consumers willing to switch to lower-cost retailers/brands
- 70% of financially stable consumers maintain brand loyalty despite economic pressures
- Gen X and Baby Boomers show lowest brand loyalty, most price-sensitive behaviors
- 30% of consumers shifting toward domestic brands due to economic/political uncertainties

**Generational Stakeholder Differences:**
- **Gen Z & Millennials:** Less motivated by basic hygiene, seek experiential benefits and mood enhancement
- **Millennials:** 26% do more online research when money is tight, focus on broader product value beyond price
- **Adults 18-44:** Drive non-hygiene motivations, value novelty and experimental features
- **Gen X & Older:** Most motivated by price, employ price-shopping strategies

**Value Perception & Purchase Motivations:**
- Gentleness and "clean" ingredients top factors for hygiene products
- Multi-benefit options prioritized for appearance-enhancing products
- Younger demographics take "broader view of product value" beyond price tag
- Online research crucial for value assessment, especially among Millennials

**Customer Engagement Cadences:**
- Adults 18-44 use personal care categories for stress relief and mood enhancement (up to 35%)
- Experimental users seek novelty even from functionally rooted categories
- Mood enhancement innovations include fragrance, sensorial benefits, and neurocosmetic formulas
- Sample sizes and discovery sets valued by experimental users

**Retention & Spending Behavior Evidence:**
- Higher spending on bodycare strongly links to increased spending in other hygiene categories
- Premiumization opportunities strongest with Gen Z and Millennials
- "Total bodycare concept" creates boom for multiple categories
- Cross-category spending behavior indicates potential for comprehensive loyalty programs

### Stakeholder Evidence Applications for Beauty Service Loyalty Programs

**Customer Value Proposition Evidence:**
- 70% of financially stable consumers resist switching to lower-cost options
- Multi-benefit offerings crucial for appearance-focused services
- Experiential and mood-enhancement benefits drive engagement among younger demographics

**Target Market Segmentation Evidence:**
- **Premium Segment:** Women 55+ (current high spenders) + Men 35-54 & Women under 55 (growth opportunities)
- **Experience-Driven Segment:** Adults 18-44 seeking stress relief and mood enhancement
- **Value-Research Segment:** Millennials who research extensively before purchasing

**Loyalty Program Design Evidence:**
- Cross-category spending behavior supports comprehensive service offerings
- Sample/discovery opportunities valued by experimental users
- Brand loyalty strong among financially stable consumers (70% retention rate)
- Domestic/local brand preference growing (30% shifting toward domestic)

**Competitive Positioning Evidence:**
- Premium positioning viable given consumer willingness to spend on quality
- Experiential benefits and mood enhancement differentiate from basic service providers
- Multi-benefit service packages align with consumer preferences
- Local/domestic positioning advantage in current market environment



**Practitioner Stakeholders**
- Instagram provides many different theories on how to increase client re-booking
- Clearly a common issue across the industry based on the amount of content allocated to fixing the problem 
- Beauty industry professionals actively share retention strategies across social media platforms
- Common practitioner-recommended retention tactics include:
  * Pre-booking next appointment during current service
  * Follow-up text reminders 24-48 hours after service
  * Seasonal service promotions (holiday packages, summer prep)
  * Referral incentive programs for existing clients
  * Personalized service recommendations based on previous visits

---
INSTRUCTIONS:
1. Start stakeholder engagement early - it often takes longer than expected
2. Be respectful of stakeholder time and provide clear value for participation  
3. Get appropriate permissions before engaging organizational stakeholders
4. Plan for lower response rates than you hope for
5. Have multiple methods to reach different types of stakeholders
