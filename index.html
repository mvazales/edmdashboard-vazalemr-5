Author: Gopalakrishnan et al. (2021)
Context: Hair salon chain, 5,544 customers, 30-month study
Key Finding: 29.5% increase in Customer Lifetime Value
Main Benefit: Reduced customer attrition (23.6% of CLV increase)
Relevance: High - directly applicable to beauty service providers

Study 2: Consumer-Brand Relationships Meta-Analysis
Author: Khamitov et al. (2019)
Context: 588 estimates from 290 studies over 24 years
Key Finding: Brand attachment drives strongest loyalty outcomes
Framework: 5 core constructs (Brand Attachment, Brand Love, Self-Brand Connection, Brand Identification, Brand Trust)
Relevance: High - identifies key loyalty drivers across industries

Study 3: Loyalty Programs vs Short-Term Promotions
Author: Lewis (2004)
Context: Online retailer, 1,058 customers, 13-month study
Key Finding: Loyalty programs more effective than short-term promotions for retention
Method: Dynamic programming model
Relevance: Moderate - online retail context differs from service providers

Key Insights for Beauty Service Providers:
- Non-tiered loyalty programs most effective for service businesses
- Emotional brand attachment crucial for long-term retention
- Loyalty programs outperform discount-based promotions
- Expected CLV increase: 29.5% based on hair salon evidence
