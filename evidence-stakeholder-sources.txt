# Stakeholder Evidence: Feedback & Perspectives
# What different groups think about client retention in the beauty industry

## Evidence Collection Summary

**Research Sources Used:**
- IBISWorld Beauty Industry Report (2025) - market data and trends
- Mintel Personal Care Consumer Report (2025) - what customers want
- Sephora Beauty Insider Case Study - large company success story
- Beautiful Co. Hair & Lashes Case Study - small salon success story
- Bureau of Labor Statistics data - employment and wage information
- Bizminer Industry Analysis - financial performance data

**Different Groups Studied:**
- **Customers:** What consumers want from beauty services
- **Industry Leaders:** Major companies and market trends
- **Beauty Service Providers:** Both large and small businesses

## What the Beauty Industry Says About Client Retention Problems

### The Business Problem Is Real

#### Industry Is Struggling Financially
- **Revenue Dropped Hard:** Industry lost 6.0% of revenue ($16.6 billion) from 2023-2024
- **More Workers, Less Money:** Employment grew 1.8% but revenue declined 6.0% - businesses are less profitable
- **Rising Costs:** Operating expenses increased from 67.73% to 68.78% of revenue

#### Customer Retention Is the Main Issue
- **Most Clients Don't Come Back:** About 67% of new clients never return after their first visit
- **Big Money at Stake:** Each client could be worth ¬£300-¬£5000 over their lifetime
- **Industry Goal:** Keep 60-75% of clients coming back within 12 months

### Loyalty Programs Actually Work in Beauty Industry

#### Market Shows Strong Growth Potential
**Industry Size and Success Stories:**
- Beauty industry worth $68.6 billion and growing 5% per year
- 454,000 people work in 167,000 beauty businesses
- Successful companies like Sephora and Ulta credit loyalty programs for their success
- Top beauty retailers get 80% of their sales from repeat customers

#### Customers Want to Stay Loyal
**What Research Shows About Consumer Behavior:**
- 70% of financially stable customers stick with brands they like, even during tough economic times
- Younger customers (Gen Z & Millennials) want experiences that make them feel good, not just basic services
- 35% of adults aged 18-44 use beauty services for stress relief and mood improvement
- 30% of consumers prefer local/domestic brands over big chains

## Real Success Stories

### Sephora Beauty Insider Program
**Big Company Success:**
- **How It Works:** Three levels of rewards (Beauty Insider, VIB, Rouge) - spend more, get better perks
- **How Long:** Started in 2007, keeps getting better over 16+ years
- **How Big:** 17 million members in North America, 25 million worldwide
- **Business Results:** 80% of Sephora's sales come from loyalty program members
- **Customer Results:** 95% of members keep coming back

### Beautiful Co. Hair & Lashes Salon
**Small Business Success:**
- **Business Size:** 16 employees, 1 location in South Carolina
- **What They Do:** Hair, extensions, lashes, eyebrows
- **Amazing Results:** 95% of clients keep coming back
- **How They Do It:** Focus on personal relationships, not just transactions
- **Success Secrets:** Pay attention to details, follow up with clients, keep everything super clean

## What Everyone Agrees On

### The Problem Is Urgent
**Financial Pressure Is Real:**
- Industry losing money (-6.0% revenue) but still hiring people (1.8% employment growth) - businesses are getting less efficient
- Costs are eating up more of the revenue (went from 67.73% to 68.78%)
- But there's still money available for solutions - businesses make 75.82% gross profit

### Customers Want to Be Loyal
**Consumer Research Shows:**
- 70% of customers with stable finances don't want to switch to cheaper options
- Younger customers (18-44) want experiences that make them feel good
- 30% of customers prefer local businesses over big chains

### Loyalty Programs Work for Any Size Business
**Both Big and Small Can Succeed:**
- Sephora (huge company) and Beautiful Co. (small salon) both got 95% of customers to come back
- Same success factors work everywhere: care about customers personally, follow up consistently, provide quality service
- Big companies need technology systems, small businesses succeed with personal touch

## How to Make It Work

### What Successful Businesses Do
1. **Build Real Relationships:** Treat customers as people, not just transactions
2. **Follow Up Consistently:** Have a system to stay in touch with customers
3. **Provide Great Service First:** You can't build loyalty on top of bad service
4. **Use Technology Smartly:** Booking systems and customer tracking help (more important for bigger businesses)
5. **Train Your Staff:** Everyone needs to know how to build customer relationships

### The Money Makes Sense
**Industry Can Afford Loyalty Programs:**
- Businesses make good profits (75.82% gross margins) so there's money to invest
- Average household spends $768 per year on personal care services
- Each loyal customer could be worth ¬£300-¬£5000 over time
- Industry is stable (1.8% employment growth) so businesses can focus on retention

### Who to Target
**Different Customer Groups Want Different Things:**
- Women 55+ already spend the most money on skincare services
- Men 35-54 and women under 55 are growth opportunities
- Younger customers want experiences that make them feel good and reduce stress
- Customers who use one service often want other services too

## What to Do Next

### Top Recommendations
**Based on All the Research:**
1. **Target Customers with Money:** Focus on financially stable customers - 70% of them stay loyal
2. **Create a Follow-up System:** Both successful case studies had consistent ways to stay in touch with customers
3. **Make Customers Feel Good:** Younger customers (18-44) want services that reduce stress and improve mood
4. **You Can Afford It:** Industry profits (75.82% gross margins) are strong enough to invest in loyalty programs
5. **Emphasize Being Local:** 30% of customers now prefer local businesses over big chains
